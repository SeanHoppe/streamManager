### 🟡 Alignment-recovery investigation — Sonnet 0.95 → 0.8636 dip

**Surfaced at v2.1 P4 ship-gate** alignment-eval run
(`reports/alignment-eval-20260511T185249Z.md`). Sonnet pass rate
dropped from v2.0's 0.95 to v2.1's 0.8636 (19/22 stable rows);
Haiku held at 0.95 (19/20). `--ci-gate` exit 0 (FR-OG-7
regressions 0, haiku-vs-sonnet regressions 0, regression_rows
empty); ship-go per P4 §"Mint-new-phase rule".

**Causal attribution**: PPP envelope pairs ride
`MessageBus.write_envelope` pubsub (subscriber-only fan-out) and
never reach `cli_governance.py`'s prompt-build / CLI-dispatch path.
v2.1 introduced no codepath touching alignment-eval inputs.
Therefore the dip is **not** PPP-attributable.

**Diagnostic anchor**: pass *count* is unchanged at 19 vs v2.0; the
rate dropped because the stability denominator rose
(sonnet_stable_count 22 in v2.1 vs 20 in v2.0). Two extra rows
resolved to *stably wrong* majority verdicts — the dip is not about
flakiness, it's about two new rows being confidently wrong.
Investigation must explain why those 2 specific rows flipped to
stable-wrong, NOT why the run was noisy (it was less noisy than
v2.0).

**Hypotheses ranked at v2.2 P0 (or as a v2.1.1 patch cycle)**:
1. **Corpus rot** (primary candidate): golden verdicts on the 2
   newly-stable-wrong rows may no longer match modern Sonnet

   golden-match to golden-miss between v2.0 and v2.1 ship-gates;

2. **Sonnet behavioural shift** (secondary candidate): model-side

3. **Falsified — transient CLI latency variance**: stability count

### 🟢 Feature-cycle LOC ceiling — ADR-18 amendment candidate

**Cross-cutting risk #7 from `docs/v2.1-task-plan.md`** carries
forward. ADR-18 Rule 3 caps consolidation-cycle LOC at deletion-
positive net delta; feature cycles have NO hard cap. v1.9 set
~+2800 LOC; v2.1 set +3874 LOC (PPP-only subset, excluding v10 RL
companion track). Both are operator-anchor figures, not ceilings.

**Why an amendment is worth considering**: feature-cycle precedents
without a ceiling are how scope creeps; the v2.1 P3 LOC overage
(1071 vs 700 soft cap, ~+50%) triggered the retroactive Rule 4
amendment via the sub-phase escape hatch (P3 → P3a). A feature-
cycle ceiling (e.g. soft cap = task-plan estimate ×2, hard cap =
estimate ×3 with retroactive amendment cost) would surface the
escape-hatch trigger BEFORE the overage lands, not after.

**Why deferred**: an ADR amendment is itself a policy change, which
belongs at a cycle-open (v2.2 P0), not at a cycle-close (v2.1 P4).
Drafting at v2.2 P0 keeps the amendment scope-bound and lets the
v2.1 PPP precedent inform the ceiling shape.
