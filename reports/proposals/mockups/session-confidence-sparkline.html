    What the operator sees + how to drive it

    • Glance: three confidence traces sit under the scope control -- one reads healthy 0.91 steady (slate), one drifting 0.71 falling (amber), one breach 0.55 below floor (red). The worst-trending lane is readable in one horizontal sweep, no dropdown cycling.
    • Paired label + color (ADR-18 M5): every lane carries a literal state WORD plus a conf 0.NN -- trend read. Color is the SECOND channel only -- the word and number carry the meaning. The dashed line is each session's hitl_floor (dashed, not color-only).
    • Click to scope: click any lane (or pick it in the native select) to call selectSession(sid) and scope every pane to that session. The active lane shows an accent edge. Try it -- the picker and the rail stay in sync.
    • Keyboard parity: each lane is a real button in the select's option order. Tab walks lane-to-lane, Enter / Space scopes. Hover-dwell (or Enter on an active lane) opens the optional observational drawer; Esc closes it. It NEVER auto-foregrounds (M2).
    • Gated: default OFF. Toggle the switch above to watch the rail (and all its subscriptions) appear and disappear -- the load-bearing beta gate.
    • Twin of #34: this is the picker-adjacent / narrow-layout twin of the shipped #34 health-sparklines. Same tri-state semantics (FLOOR 0.6 / DRIFT 0.72), same mock seeds -- #28's only delta is PLACEMENT next to the picker for the operator driving by the dropdown.
