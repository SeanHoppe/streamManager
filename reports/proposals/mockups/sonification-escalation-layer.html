What this is

The monitor-first operator runs StreamManager on a side screen while working elsewhere. A purely visual ACTION-REQUIRED pulse is invisible on an unwatched screen. This turns the dashboard into a pre-attentive summons: when a foreground-eligible escalation enters the existing escalationStore, the controller plays the type's distinct tone. Ear pulls attention -- glance -- read the paired badge -- act.

Sound is the summons, the badge is the signal. The tone stops the moment the badge clears (the same 20s ESC_WINDOW the Frame-A foreground already uses), so silence == handled. Sound can never be the lone signal: a deaf or muted operator loses nothing -- the visible badge stays fully sufficient.

Try it

1. Press Preview on any row to learn that type's tone deliberately (on your terms, not during a real incident).

2. Press Simulate burst -- 5 mock escalations enter in sequence; each plays its grammar tone and the window bar drains over ~6s (compressed from 20s for the demo), then the badge clears and the tone stops.

3. Toggle Master mute ("I'm in a meeting, hush"), flip a row OFF, or flip the Feature gate OFF -- play() is suppressed with no error and (gate OFF) the subscriber tears down entirely.
