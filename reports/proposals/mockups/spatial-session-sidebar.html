The glance (return-to-desk scan)

Four claude -p sessions live; you stepped away for coffee. The left rail says "3 open actions across 4 sessions" and four near-identical slate lanes. The question in your head is not "how many" -- it is "which one FIRST, and is anything spreading?"

Your eye lands on the right rail. The BLOCK-ringed gamma-lib node with a rising sparkline and a pulsing STATIC RULE outline is the one on fire. The thick x4 edge between alpha-svc and beta-app says those two converged on 4 of the same learned patterns. No click needed. One Focus and Frame A swings to it.

Calm + safety contract

  • Read-only GETs only -- /api/sessions/spatial-overview + /api/sessions/pattern-edges. No new bus envelope, no frozen surface touched (frozenTouch:false). Both mirror the polarity-filtered /api/sessions/health-digest aggregation.
  • G2 polarity: server filters project_slug NOT IN sm_slugs AND session_id != SM_OWN_SESSION_ID; the panel footer surfaces excluded_self (always >=1, even in mock).
  • M16: identity is rendered FROM DATA (project_slug) -- placeholder names only (alpha-svc / beta-app / ...), no monitored-project vocabulary.
  • M2: the escalation pulse is an outline animation (no color-only change) and respects prefers-reduced-motion + the FR-UI-9 data-motion override.
  • Flag OFF => the whole sidebar renders nothing and registers no fetch (no-op gate). Flip the switch to see it.

Keyboard + interaction path (M17)

  • Nodes are real buttons in DOM order (most-recent first) -- Tab walks them.
  • Arrow keys move selection within the field; Enter / Space = Focus that session (calls the same guarded selectSession(id) the left rail uses -- structurally refuses SM-self).
  • Esc collapses the panel to the 28px spine (a button aria-expanded with a paired chevron + "MAP").
  • Scroll = zoom, drag-background = pan; collapsed/expanded + zoom/pan persist to localStorage.
  • On narrow viewports (<55rem, the AppShell left-rail breakpoint) the 2D field reprojects to a 1D horizontal timeline strip -- same nodes, same badges, ordered by last_activity. Try the 1D strip toggle.
