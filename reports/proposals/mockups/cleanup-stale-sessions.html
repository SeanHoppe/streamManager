Reclaim a lean, trustworthy SessionRail -- without hand-written SQL

Over a long day the rail accretes dead non-SM lanes -- exited claude -p runs, crashed PIDs, one-off probes -- that crowd the calm rail and dilute the ACTIVE tally. Today the only purge is to alt-tab to a terminal and hand-write DELETE against gov.db: irreversible, scary, and a context switch out of the monitor.

This BETA is the HEAVIER sibling of stale-cleanup (#46). The honest delta is two things #46 does not do:

  1. Hands-off calm-away auto-purge. Tick Enable daily cleanup at 02:00 UTC once; the rail purges itself overnight and you never reach for it again.
  2. A forensic audit log. Every deletion writes a cleanup_log row + a cleanup_event -- proof of WHAT was removed and WHEN.

Manual mode is glance -> 1 click: the banner above surfaces when >0 exited sessions are >=30 min stale. Unlike #46's reversible soft-delete, this hard-deletes message / decision / hitl rows >24h -- the confirm copy says so plainly so you never confuse the two BETAs.

Paired label + color badge scheme (ADR-18 M4)

Every state is a text+color pair. The colored dot is decoration; the literal label carries the signal -- a color-blind operator never depends on the dot.
